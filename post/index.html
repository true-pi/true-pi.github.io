What is wrong with textbook Pi ?

It all begins well over 2000 years ago (if you can believe history textbooks) in ancient Greece, with a man named Archimedes. He devised a geometrical method of finding approximate value of Pi, by calculating perimeters of regular n-sided polygons. The idea was basically this: put one n-sided polygon that just fits inside the circle (inscribed polygon), and put another polygon that circumscribes the circle (fits around it). The circumference of the circle will be somewhere between perimeter of inner and outer polygons.
